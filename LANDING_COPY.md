## FAQ — 5 Questions a Skeptical CTO Would Ask

### 1. How do you know your pricing data is accurate?

Every price in our engine is hardcoded from the official vendor pricing page, converted to INR using a fixed May 7, 2026 reference rate (1 USD = Rs 94.37). We do not scrape or estimate. The ChatGPT Business price uses OpenAI's official India-localized rate (Rs 1,800/seat/mo annual). Our source URLs are documented in [PRICING_DATA.md](./PRICING_DATA.md) — you can verify every number yourself.

### 2. What do you do with my email?

We use your work email for one purpose: to send you a follow-up with your audit report link. If your audit surfaces significant savings (>Rs 40,000/month), we may reach out to offer a free consultation. We never sell your email to third parties, and you can request deletion at any time.

### 3. Why should I trust a savings estimate from a tool that wants to sell me consulting?

Because our Honesty Filter is built into the engine. If your monthly savings are below Rs 8,350 (~$100), the report explicitly says "You're spending well" and does not push a consultation CTA. We lose potential leads by being honest — and that's the point. Trust is our conversion mechanism, not hype.

### 4. Can this handle API usage (token-based pricing), or only subscriptions?

Both. For subscription tools (Cursor, Copilot, Claude, ChatGPT), we calculate based on your plan and seat count. For API tools (Anthropic API, OpenAI API), we use an estimated "moderate usage" profile (10M input + 2M output tokens/month) and calculate your cost using verified per-token rates. The audit shows the exact math.

### 5. Why INR? We're a US-based startup.

The tool is built for Indian and Southeast Asian startups where AI tool pricing is often confusing because vendors price in USD but teams budget in local currency. The INR-native approach eliminates runtime exchange rate fluctuations and gives founders a stable number they can put directly into their P&L. USD support is planned for v2.
